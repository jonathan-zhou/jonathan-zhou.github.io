• What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to manage and understand one’s emotions/feelings and turn them into positive or meaningful ways to manage stress, communication with peers, difficulties or obstacles in life, etc. You can use that emotional information to guide one’s thoughts and behaviour.

• How is it different to IQ?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) is a measure of one’s intelligence while EQ (Emotional Quotient) is a measure of one’s emotions. Being able to manage and identify the emotions of one’s self and others. IQ by itself can only get someone so far as they are more limited when it comes working in groups of people. Research has shown that EQ is known to foster more productivity and workflow as a person with High EQ is able to work adaptively and respond more effectively to changing conditions. It has also been shown that effective leaders have very developed EQ skills.

• Why is it important?

A developed EQ can help foster better productivity as well as better relationships with peers. People are able to recognize each other’s emotions and are able to act accordingly to the situation which means they are more capable to change. It is also possible to create environments where more productivity thanks to EQ.
